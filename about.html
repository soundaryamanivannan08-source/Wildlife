The Journey

How Stackly WildJourneys Began

Founded in 2012 by a small team of passionate Tanzanian guides and international conservationists, WildJourneys was born from a desire to redefine luxury eco-tourism. We noticed safari models that left local communities out of the benefits and ignored basic conservation principles.

We started with just two modified Land Cruisers and a lease on a single community concession in the Serengeti. By pairing high-end hospitality with raw bush knowledge and community-first practices, we proved that travelers would invest in authentic, ethical adventures.

"We do not inherit the earth from our ancestors, we borrow it from our children."

Today, we operate in 4 African nations, employing over 120 local guides, hospitality team members, and rangers. Our footprint is carbon neutral, but our core mission remains unchanged: showing guests the raw power of nature while ensuring its survival for generations to come.

Our Evolution

Milestones Along the Way

2012

The Spark

WildJourneys was founded with just two modified Land Cruisers and a lease on a single community concession in the Serengeti, aiming to prove tourism can directly benefit conservation.

2016

The Empowerment Shift

Transitioned our primary operations to community-owned models. Local tribal councils received direct land-lease dividends, empowering indigenous communities to protect their heritage.

2021

Going Carbon Neutral

We converted all mobile and permanent camps to 100% solar power grids and established a verified carbon-offset program for all internal and client safari transit.

2026

Expanding Horizons

Proudly launching marine wildlife conservation safaris on the coast of Mozambique, protecting critical coral reefs and marine life while supporting coastal villages.
